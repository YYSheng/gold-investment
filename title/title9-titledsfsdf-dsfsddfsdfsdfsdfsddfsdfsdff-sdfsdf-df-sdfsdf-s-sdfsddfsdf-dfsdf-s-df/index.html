The Fed may shrink its balance sheet in May, gold prices rose and fell strongly

International gold closed down strongly on Tuesday (April 5), with the opening price of $1,933.47/oz, the highest price of $1,944.33/oz, the lowest price of $1,918.93/oz, and the closing price of $1,920.62/oz.

Message side:

The U.S. March ISM non-manufacturing PMI released on Tuesday was 58.3, lower than market expectations of 58.4, and the previous value was 56.5; the final value of the U.S. Markit services PMI in March was 58, lower than market expectations of 58.9.

Commented that this is another strong report. The rebound in employment and the rise in new orders underscored a strong rebound in the services sector, which was expected in the post-pandemic era. This is the 22nd consecutive month that the index has exceeded 50.

S&P Global Chief Business Economist Chris Williamson commented that business activity in the huge services sector was boosted by the easing of epidemic restrictions in March, and has regained its strong momentum since the slowdown caused by the epidemic at the beginning of the year. Demand for the service has grown so fast that it has become increasingly difficult for businesses to keep up with the increase in customer orders, leading to the largest increase in data since records began in 2009. However, while companies have a good order record to maintain strong output in the coming months, prices will face further upward pressure as demand outstrips supply. As the prices of energy, fuel and other raw materials skyrocket and wages rise, so do the costs for businesses, and service prices are rising at an unprecedented rate. As a result, consumer price inflation is likely to accelerate further heading into spring.

Fed Governor Brainard said the Fed could adjust policy at each meeting. The Fed is likely to rapidly shrink its balance sheet as soon as May. The Fed is prepared to take more forceful action if indicators of inflation and inflation expectations suggest such action is warranted. Inflation is currently too high and vulnerable to upside risks. Long-term inflation expectations remain within historical ranges. A 50 basis point hike is an option we have to consider. There is “no doubt” that easing must be lifted. Will see GDP fall and fiscal policy shrink. May’s adjustment needs to go beyond neutral policy to keep inflation in check.

Fed official Daly said he will be able to reduce inflation by the end of the year, but there will be no recession. Economic growth will decelerate, but only temporarily. There will be more supply chain issues for the rest of the year, but we will see some signs of recovery. An improvement in the global pandemic situation will ease supply chain issues. In addition to raising interest rates, the Fed will also reduce policy easing by reducing its balance sheet. Balance sheet reduction could begin as soon as the May meeting. I don’t think the U.S. economy will slow down significantly because of high oil prices. Most of the imbalances are related to the new coronavirus.

ECB Chief Economist Len said he saw a decline in different indicators of investor confidence and consumer confidence because people were nervous amid the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Economic growth is slowing down for now, but the economy will still grow this year.

Geographical situation:

The United States and the G7 will announce sweeping new sanctions on Russia on Wednesday, according to NBC News. New U.S. sanctions against Russia will include a ban on new investments in Russia. New U.S. sanctions against Russia also target Russian individuals and their families.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky said that Russia’s purpose is to provoke a global food crisis. Urge the UN Security Council to hold Russia fully accountable for its actions in Ukraine. Calls for Russia to be expelled from the UN Security Council.

Ukrainian officials say diplomatic talks with Russia must continue for the sake of peace. There was no compromise on ceding Ukrainian territory.

The position of SPDR Gold Trust, the world’s largest gold ETF, decreased by 2.68 tons compared with the previous day, and the current position is 1087.3 tons.

According to CME’s “Federal Reserve Watch”: the probability of the Fed raising interest rates by 25 basis points by May is 22.9%, the probability of raising interest rates by 50 basis points is 77.7%, and the probability of raising interest rates by 75 basis points is 0%; There is a 0% probability of a 100 basis point hike or a 50 basis point rate hike, a 16.7% chance of a 75 basis point rate hike, a 62.4% chance of a 100 basis point rate hike, and a 20.9% chance of a 125 basis point rate hike.
