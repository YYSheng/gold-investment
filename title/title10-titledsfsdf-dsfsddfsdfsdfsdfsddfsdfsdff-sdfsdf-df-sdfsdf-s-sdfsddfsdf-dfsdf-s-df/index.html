Spot gold and silver tested the first support, WTI crude oil concerned about 97.09 support

The Fed’s hawkish minutes and new sanctions on Russia again added to market volatility. Spot gold remained volatile on Wednesday. After the Fed minutes were released, it rose first and then fell, closing slightly up 0.09% at $1,925.26 per ounce; spot silver edged higher in the US session, closing up 0.5% at $24.46 per ounce.

Affected by the IEA’s confirmation of the release of 240 million barrels, WTI crude oil closed down 3.96% at US$97.9 per barrel, while Brent crude oil once fell 5% and closed down 3.87% at US$103.92 per barrel.

The minutes of the Fed’s March meeting showed that officials believed that it may be appropriate to reduce the asset ceiling by $95 billion ($60 billion in Treasury bonds + $35 billion in MBS) per month, and supported a phased adjustment of 3 months or a moderately longer period to achieve the reduction. Table caps, and the process could begin as early as May. In addition, if the price pressure does not come down, one or more rate hikes of 50 basis points may be required.

Currently, money market traders are betting the Fed will raise rates by 225 basis points by the end of the year. Including the rate hikes already implemented in March, this will mean a cumulative rate hike of 250 basis points throughout the year, and the tightening will be the highest in nearly 30 years.

But analysts say gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset and an inflation hedge offsets the Fed’s hawkish expectations. Bob Haberkorn, senior market strategist at RJO Futures, said gold prices are likely to continue to rise over the next two quarters as the Federal Reserve cannot raise interest rates fast enough to combat high inflation. However, the dollar surged to a near two-year high, undermining gold’s appeal.

OANDA senior market analyst Craig Erlam said that there are still many factors that could trigger gold to rise again, such as inflation continuing to exceed current expectations, the breakdown of Russia-Ukraine talks or a recession.

In terms of the situation in Russia and Ukraine, the Kremlin said on Wednesday that the progress of peace talks between Moscow and Kyiv was not as rapid or positive as expected. The United States announced additional sanctions against Russian financial institutions and the daughter of Russian President Vladimir Putin. The new round of sanctions includes the Russian Foreign Minister and his wife in addition to Putin’s daughter.

In addition, the United States, the European Union and G7 countries will jointly announce a ban on new investment in Russia. U.K., U.S. will impose sanctions on Sberbank. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky continued to call on allied countries to strengthen international sanctions against Russia. So far, the United States has shipped 100 Switchblade drones to Ukraine. U.S. President Joe Biden said the U.S. continues to supply Ukraine with arms and resources.
